An unihabitable rock named after the Roman God of [well, a lot of things depending on who you ask!], Mercury is a crater-laden chunk of rock and iron. Not the nicest place in the galaxy, it's under perpetual assult from extreme solar radiation; adding to this, Mercury has next to no atmosphere to protect it from comets and asteroids. The planet was visited by the Marniner 10 and Messanger spacecrafts.

Planet Earth, the wild and wacky home of humankind. Though its inhabitants once thought it to be the center of the universe, it is merely a beautiful blip in a quiet galaxy, no more than a drop in the galactic sea. Oddly enough, its wisest humans came to understand their cosmic insignificance, and, in classic human fashion, refused to accept this obvious fact. With unbelievable grit and determination, they developed objects to closely examine the star filled darkness of night; they engineered vessels to explore nearby worlds and launch themselves to the moon; perhaps one day, Earth's inhabitants might even come to call another planet home!
